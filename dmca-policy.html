📜 DMCA & Copyright Policy

Last updated: 13 July 2026 · Rheoa RPG

Rheoa RPG (“we”, “us”, or “our”) respects the intellectual property rights of others and expects our users to do the same. In accordance with the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA) and similar international copyright laws, we have established the following policy for handling claims of copyright infringement on our platform.

This policy applies to all content posted by users on Rheoa, including but not limited to forum posts, comments, testimonials, profile information, gang names, and any other user‑generated content.

⚖️ Safe Harbor: We are committed to complying with the DMCA’s safe harbor provisions. Upon receipt of a valid takedown notice, we will act expeditiously to remove or disable access to the allegedly infringing material.

1. Filing a DMCA Takedown Notice

If you believe that your copyrighted work has been copied and is available on Rheoa in a way that constitutes copyright infringement, you may submit a takedown notice to our designated Copyright Agent. The notice must include the following information (as required by 17 U.S.C. § 512(c)(3)):

  • Physical or electronic signature of a person authorised to act on behalf of the copyright owner.
  • Identification of the copyrighted work claimed to have been infringed (or a representative list if multiple works).
  • Identification of the material that is claimed to be infringing or to be the subject of infringing activity – with enough detail for us to locate it (e.g., the exact URL or post ID).
  • Your contact information – name, address, telephone number, and email address.
  • A statement that you have a good faith belief that the use of the material is not authorised by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.
  • A statement that the information in the notification is accurate, and under penalty of perjury, that you are authorised to act on behalf of the copyright owner.

Please send your notice to our designated Copyright Agent:

  • Email: copyright@rheoa.com
  • Postal Address: [Your Business Address – replace with actual address]

Important: Submitting a false or misleading takedown notice may result in legal liability. Please ensure that your claim is legitimate before contacting us.

2. Counter‑Notification

If you believe that material you posted on Rheoa was removed or disabled by mistake or misidentification, you may submit a counter‑notification. The counter‑notification must include:

  • Your signature (physical or electronic).
  • Identification of the material that has been removed or disabled and the location where it appeared before removal.
  • A statement under penalty of perjury that you have a good faith belief that the material was removed or disabled as a result of mistake or misidentification.
  • Your name, address, and telephone number, and a statement that you consent to the jurisdiction of the federal court in your district (or the courts in England and Wales, if you are outside the US) and that you will accept service of process from the person who filed the original takedown notice.

Send your counter‑notification to the same contact details above. After receiving a valid counter‑notification, we may forward a copy to the original complainant and restore the material unless we receive notice that the complainant has filed a court action to restrain the infringement.

🌍 Global Considerations: While this policy is based on US DMCA requirements, we apply similar principles to copyright claims from all jurisdictions. If you are outside the US, please provide equivalent details so we can process your request efficiently.

3. Repeat Infringer Policy

In accordance with the DMCA and other applicable laws, we maintain a policy of terminating, in appropriate circumstances, the accounts of repeat infringers. We define a repeat infringer as a user who has been the subject of three (3) or more valid takedown notices within a 12‑month period, provided that those notices were not successfully contested via counter‑notification.

If a user is identified as a repeat infringer, we reserve the right to:

  • Issue a formal warning.
  • Temporarily suspend the account.
  • Permanently terminate the account (without refund of any virtual currency or items).

We also reserve the right to take action against users who engage in blatant or egregious copyright infringement, even if they have not yet reached three strikes.

4. How We Respond to Notices

Upon receipt of a valid takedown notice:

  • We will remove or disable access to the allegedly infringing material as quickly as possible (typically within 1–3 business days).
  • We will notify the user who posted the material that it has been removed, and provide them with a copy of the notice.
  • If the user submits a valid counter‑notification, we will consider reinstating the material after the statutory waiting period (usually 10–14 business days), unless we receive notice of a court action.

We may also, at our discretion, forward the takedown notice to the user who posted the material, even if they are located outside the US, to ensure transparency and provide them an opportunity to respond.

5. Misuse of the DMCA Process

Submitting false or abusive takedown notices or counter‑notifications is a serious matter. We reserve the right to take appropriate action against individuals who misuse the DMCA process, including reporting such conduct to law enforcement and terminating the offending account.
